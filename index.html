Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 10, 2026

At Sure Safe, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our services, including our internal automation tools, data processing services, and web-based applications.

Important: By using our services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our services.

1. Scope and Application

This Privacy Policy applies to all services, tools, and platforms operated by Sure Safe, including:

  • Internal automation tools and scripts
  • Business intelligence dashboards and reporting systems
  • Data processing workflows and integrations
  • Web-based applications and APIs
  • Third-party platform integrations

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Business Information

We collect and process business-related information necessary to provide our services:

  • Company name and business entity information
  • Business contact details (email addresses, phone numbers, business addresses)
  • Billing and payment information
  • Tax identification numbers and related documentation

2.2 Transaction Data

  • Order details and transaction metadata
  • Purchase history and order status
  • Shipping and logistics information
  • Delivery tracking and confirmation data

2.3 Technical Information

  • System-generated identifiers and session tokens
  • Timestamps and audit logs
  • IP addresses and device information
  • Usage analytics and performance metrics

3. How We Use Your Information

Sure Safe processes your information only for legitimate business purposes, including:

  • Service Delivery: Processing orders, managing fulfillment, and providing customer support
  • Financial Operations: Billing, invoicing, payment processing, and financial reconciliation
  • Compliance: Meeting tax obligations, regulatory requirements, and legal mandates
  • Business Analytics: Operational reporting, performance monitoring, and business intelligence
  • Process Improvement: Optimizing workflows, automating business processes, and enhancing service quality
  • Security: Detecting fraud, preventing unauthorized access, and maintaining system integrity

4. Data Minimization and Privacy by Design

We adhere to the principle of data minimization. We only collect and process the minimum amount of information necessary to accomplish a specific, legitimate purpose. Data collected for one purpose is not repurposed for unrelated activities without appropriate legal basis and notification.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your information based on the following legal grounds:

  • Contractual Necessity: Processing required to fulfill our contractual obligations
  • Legal Obligation: Compliance with applicable laws and regulations
  • Legitimate Interest: Pursuing our legitimate business interests while respecting your rights
  • Consent: Where you have provided explicit consent for specific processing activities

6. Data Security Measures

6.1 Encryption

We implement industry-standard encryption to protect your information:

  • Data at rest: AES-256 encryption or equivalent
  • Data in transit: TLS 1.2 or higher protocols
  • Secure key management infrastructure

6.2 Access Controls

Access to information is restricted through comprehensive security measures:

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) systems
  • Principle of least privilege enforcement
  • Multi-factor authentication for sensitive systems
  • Regular access rights reviews and audits
  • Immediate revocation of access upon role changes or termination

6.3 Infrastructure Security

  • Secure cloud-based infrastructure with certified providers
  • Network segmentation and firewall protection
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability scanning
  • Automated backup systems with encryption

7. Data Retention and Deletion

We retain your information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.

  • Transaction Data: Retained for the duration required by tax and accounting regulations (typically 7 years)
  • Operational Logs: Retained for 12-24 months unless required for ongoing investigations
  • Personal Information: Deleted or anonymized when no longer necessary for business purposes

Upon expiration of retention periods, data is securely deleted using methods that prevent recovery or reconstruction.

8. Development and Testing Practices

To protect your privacy during system development and testing:

  • Testing is conducted in isolated, non-production environments
  • Production data is anonymized, masked, or synthetic when used for testing
  • Strict controls prevent unauthorized data migration between environments
  • Development access to production systems is logged and monitored

9. Monitoring, Logging, and Auditing

We maintain comprehensive monitoring and logging systems to ensure security and compliance:

  • Automated detection of unauthorized access attempts
  • Real-time security alerts and anomaly detection
  • Audit trails for all data access and modifications
  • Regular review of system logs and access patterns
  • Compliance reporting and documentation

10. Incident Response and Breach Notification

In the event of a security incident or data breach, Sure Safe follows a documented incident response procedure:

  • Detection and Assessment: Immediate identification and evaluation of the incident
  • Containment: Rapid isolation of affected systems and restriction of unauthorized access
  • Investigation: Root cause analysis and impact assessment
  • Remediation: Implementation of corrective measures and security enhancements
  • Notification: Timely notification to affected parties and relevant authorities as required by law
  • Post-Incident Review: Analysis of response effectiveness and process improvements

11. Third-Party Service Providers

We may engage trusted third-party service providers to support our operations. These providers are carefully selected based on their:

  • Security certifications and compliance standards
  • Data protection practices and policies
  • Track record of reliability and reputation
  • Contractual commitments to data security

Third-party providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes we specify. We do not sell or rent your information to third parties.

12. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your information:

  • Access: Request access to the information we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Data Portability: Request a copy of your information in a structured format
  • Objection: Object to certain processing activities
  • Restriction: Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided below.

13. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. When we transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Standard contractual clauses approved by regulatory authorities
  • Data transfer agreements with third-party providers
  • Compliance with applicable data protection laws

14. Children's Privacy

Our services are intended for business use and are not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete that information.

15. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy
  • Notify you through appropriate channels (email, system notification, or website notice)
  • Provide a reasonable period for review before changes take effect (where required by law)

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

16. Complaints and Regulatory Authority

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated or you are not satisfied with our response to your inquiry, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority or supervisory body.
